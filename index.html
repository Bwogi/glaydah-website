About Glaydah

Glaydah Namukasa was born and raised in Uganda.

She is a finalist on the MFA program in Creative Writing at University of California Riverside where she is recipient of the Dean’s Distinguished Fellowship Award. She holds a BS in Community Psychology from Makerere University, Uganda. She is a member of FEMRITE. Her publications include one novel, The Deadly Ambition, 2006 and a young-adult novella, Voice of a Dream which was awarded the Macmillan Writers Prize for Africa in 2006. Her short stories have been published in Uganda, South Africa, the UK, the US, and Sweden. She is a recipient of the Rockefeller Foundation Bellagio Center Residency 2013, Mike and Marylee Fairbanks Fellowship 2006, Art Omi International Residency 2009, City of Asylum, Pittsburgh, and in 2008 she was awarded the title of Honorary Fellow by the International Writing Program University of Iowa. In 2014 she was selected on the Hay Festival Africa39 list of writers to influence future trends in African Literature. April 2018 she received the URSB award for Ugandan Women in Literary Creativity. She has mentored Ugandan writers, two of whom have published books. She has been Chair person of FEMRITE, Uganda Women Writers Association 2014-2017.

Her second novel is forth coming. She is signed up under the Chandler Crawford Agency INC.

Awards & Recognitions

The Abraham L. Polonsky Award

Awarded as an outstanding Creative Writer by the department of Creative Writing at the University of California, Riverside in 2020.

Macmillan Prize for African Writers(2006)

Glaydah won this prize in the Young Adult category for her book, Voice of A Dream competed for by Africans in Africa and those in the diaspora.

International Affairs Recognition Award(2020), UCR

Awarded by the International Students and Scholars | International Affairs department in 2020 for her outstanding contributions to University of California, Riverside. Glaydah was noted as “serious, diligent, thoughtful, and very talented. Of equal importance, she has become a standard-bearer for her entire cohort, setting the pace in writing courses and seminars for critical engagement and creativity.”

Hay Festival Africa39 List

Selected in 2014 on the Hay Festival Africa39 list of writers under the age of 40 from Sub-Saharan Africa and the diaspora destined to influence future trends in African Literature. The writers were selected by Margaret Busby (Ghana/UK), Elechi Amadi (Nigeria) and Osonye Tess Onwueme (Nigeria/US). The writers feature in the Africa39 anthology, Bloomsbury publishing.

URSB Women in Creativity National Award(2018)

Awarded in April 2018 by the Uganda Registration Services Bureau as one of the Women whose Creativity is Powering Change through their Literary Works.

Shortlisted, The Susie Smith Memorial Prize for HIV/AIDS stories, Oxfam GB(2008)

Her short Non-fiction piece, “The Second Twin” was shortlisted for the Prize for HIV/AIDS stories by Oxfam. The Second Twin is published in “I Dare to Say” Anthology, FEMRITE publications, June 2004.

Shortlisted, Ken Sarowiwa Legacy Anthology

Shortlisted for the Ken Sarowiwa Legacy Anthology, 2004 for her poem, 'Yet Hope Survives', published in Sable Literary Magazine, UK.
